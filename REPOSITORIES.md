## Boundary Rule

Public:

— released texts;
— research notes approved for publication;
— public methodologies;
— public diagrams and tables;
— public document templates;
— public version history;
— public issues and discussions used as publication evidence.

Private:

— drafts not ready for publication;
— internal hypotheses not yet approved;
— raw notes and raw measurements;
— sensitive operational details;
— personal data;
— credentials and access instructions;
— legal, financial, partner, or security-sensitive coordination;
— anything that should not become part of the public record.

## Publication Rule

Nothing moves from private to public automatically.

Before publication:

1. Review for privacy, secrets, partner data, and operational sensitivity.
2. Decide whether the material is research text, data, code, model, or engineering system.
3. Apply the correct license.
4. Add attribution to Landau Fund.
5. Link the original public repository.
6. Indicate changes if adapting previous material.
7. Update `CHANGELOG.md`.
8. Create a public issue or discussion when a publication record is needed.
